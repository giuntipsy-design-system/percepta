Guidance

Accessibility

Accessibility in Percepta starts with design decisions, not with technical fixes. It is not an additional step applied at the end of the process, but a way of thinking embedded in how interfaces are structured, labeled, and explained.

Design choices such as hierarchy, clarity, spacing, and feedback have a direct impact on accessibility. These decisions are made every day by designers and developers, long before code-level considerations come into play.

Accessibility as a design and development mindset

Percepta treats accessibility as an integral part of the design and development process. It is considered from the first sketches and wireframes through to implementation and iteration.

Designers establish clear structure, intent, and language. Developers ensure that these decisions are implemented correctly and consistently. Accessibility emerges from this continuous collaboration, not from isolated fixes.

Shared responsibility

Accessibility in Percepta is a shared responsibility between the design system, the underlying framework, and the teams building the product.

Percepta defines semantic structure, usage patterns, and visual consistency. The framework provides keyboard interaction, focus management, and ARIA behavior. Product teams are responsible for context, content, and correct implementation.

What Percepta guarantees

  • Components follow native semantics whenever possible.
  • Interaction patterns are consistent across the system.
  • Keyboard navigation is supported and documented.
  • Visual states do not rely on color alone.
  • Accessibility considerations are documented at the component and pattern level.

Notes on compliance

Percepta aims to support compliance with accessibility standards such as WCAG. However, compliance depends on correct usage and context-specific implementation within each product.
