Usage

Textareas are designed for longer, free-form text input. They are best suited for content that requires multiple lines, editing, or review.

Use a textarea when the amount of text cannot be predicted or when users need space to structure their thoughts. For short or predictable input, a single-line input provides a clearer and more efficient interaction.

Guidelines

  • Use a clear, descriptive label that explains what the field captures.
  • Place validation messages close to the textarea and tie them to the field.
  • Use textareas intentionally to avoid overwhelming dense interfaces.
  • Provide context when a textarea appears without a visible label.

Keyboard support

  • Tab moves focus to the textarea.
  • Enter creates new lines inside the field.
  • Text editing follows native multiline input behavior.

Accessibility

Ensure each textarea has a visible label and accessible error messaging. Native semantics and ARIA are handled by the framework.
